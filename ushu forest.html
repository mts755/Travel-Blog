Ushu Forest: Captivating forest on heights.

Usho (also spelled Ushu) is a village in Usho Valley, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa province of Pakistan. It is situated 8 kilometers (5.0 mi) from Kalam and 123 kilometers (76 mi) km from Mingora, at the height of 2,300 meters (7,550 feet). It is accessible through a non metalled road from Kalam by jeeps only. Usho is known for its beautiful cloudy and rainy forest. Tourist attraction Mahodand lake is located 27 kilometers (17 mi) from there. In winter, the famous Ushu glacier blocks the only path to the Mahodand lake and this path is only opened again in Summer, when the snow of the glacier melts and the authorities wipe of the remaining snow from the road. Ushu Forest is a destination for camping and nature-viewing in a serene, lush forest with some meadows. The forest allows you to witness unique natural wonders along with the biodiversity of Upper Swat. Situated 8 kilometers (5.0 mi) from Kalam, Ushu is known for its beautiful rainforests.
